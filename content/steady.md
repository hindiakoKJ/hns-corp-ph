## The Problem

**Heading:**  
Living with epilepsy means living with uncertainty.

You never know when the next one will happen. You can't always remember what happened after. Your caregiver might not be in the room. And every few months, you sit in a neurologist's office trying to reconstruct the last six months from memory.

Steady doesn't cure any of that. But it helps you stop relying on memory alone.

---

## What Steady Does

**Section label:** Four things, done well

**Card 1 — Log it**  
When you feel one coming, tap once. Steady records the time, your location, and the current weather. When it ends, it records how long it lasted. No typing. No writing. Just tap.

**Card 2 — Alert your people**  
BEACON sends a message to every emergency contact at the same time — SMS and push notification, with a Google Maps link to where you are. One tap. Everyone who needs to know, knows.

**Card 3 — Show your doctor**  
Steady generates a PDF report: a six-month chart, your seizure count and average duration, and a full log with time, weather, and notes. Print it. Send it. Bring it to your next appointment.

**Card 4 — Know when it might happen**  
Steady's accelerometer watches for rhythmic motion (3–8 Hz shaking) and prompts you to log. It's a supplementary tool — not a medical diagnostic device, and the app says so clearly. But it might catch what you miss.

## BEACON

**Heading:**  
One tap. Everyone knows.

When you fire BEACON, Steady:
- Sends a push notification to every contact with the app installed
- Sends an SMS to every contact with a phone number
- Includes a Google Maps link to your current location
- Uses your patient nickname — not your legal name

You don't have to call anyone. You don't have to type anything. You just tap.

**Sub:**  
Contacts are household-scoped. Phone numbers are optional — if a contact only has the app, they still get notified.

## Bystander Mode

**Heading:**  
Your phone can help a stranger.

Bystander Mode is a public first-aid guide — no login, no account. If someone near you is having a seizure and they have Steady on their phone, you can open it and see exactly what to do: recovery position, how to time it, what not to do, when to call emergency services.

**Steps (summary):**
1. Stay calm
2. Recovery position
3. Time it
4. Clear the area
5. Don't restrain
6. Don't put anything in their mouth

## Privacy Promise

**Heading:**  
What we store, and what we don't.

Steady was designed with privacy as a constraint, not a policy.

- **No full names.** Patients are stored as nicknames. Your legal name is not in our database.
- **Phone numbers are optional.** Contacts don't need a phone number to receive alerts.
- **No advertising.** Steady is free. It always will be. We don't monetize your health data.
- **Your data stays yours.** We don't share it with insurers, employers, or anyone else.

## For Your Neurologist

**Heading:**  
Bring something real to your next appointment.

Instead of "I think I had about four last month," bring a PDF:

- Six-month seizure frequency chart
- Total count, average duration, longest event
- Full log with timestamps, weather, temperature, and your notes
- Shareable via your phone's share sheet — email, print, or message it directly

Steady generates this on your phone. There's no email backend. No cloud processing. It's yours.
